## Prerequisites

Before you begin, ensure you have:

<Callout kind="info">
- A modern web browser like Chrome, Firefox, or Safari
- An email address for account verification
- Optional: Existing Markdown files or GitHub repos for import
</Callout>

## Create Your Account

Follow these steps to sign up for SOURADEEP MUKHERJEE.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit the Dashboard" icon="monitor">
    Navigate to `https://dashboard.example.com` in your browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start Registration" icon="user-plus">
    Click **Sign Up** and enter your email address and a strong password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Email" icon="mail">
    Check your inbox for a verification email from SOURADEEP MUKHERJEE and click the link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete Setup" icon="check-circle">
    Return to the dashboard and set your workspace name, such as `My Projects`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Log In and Access Your Workspace

Once registered, log in to begin.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Browser" icon="globe">
    Go to `https://dashboard.example.com/login`, enter your credentials, and select your workspace.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Bookmark" icon="bookmark">
    Bookmark `https://dashboard.example.com` for quick access and use **Remember Me** for convenience.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Create Your First Document

Build your first documentation page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="New Document" icon="file-plus">
    In the workspace sidebar, click **New Page** and name it `Quickstart`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit Content" icon="edit-3">
    Use the MDX editor to add content. Here's a starter template:
    
    <CodeGroup tabs="MDX,YAML">
      ```mdx
      ---
      title: My First Page
      description: Getting started.
      ---

      ## Welcome

      This is your first document.
      ```
      ```yaml
      title: My First Page
      description: Getting started.
      ```
    </CodeGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview and Publish" icon="eye">
    Click **Preview** to view changes, then **Publish** to make it live.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">
  Use the rich text toolbar for headings, lists, and embeds to speed up editing.
</Callout>

## Import Existing Content

Bring in your current docs seamlessly.

<Expandable title="Import from GitHub" default-open="true">
  1. Go to **Settings > Imports**.
  2. Connect your GitHub account.
  3. Select a repo and branch, like `main`.
  4. Map files to your workspace structure.

  Example repo structure:
  ```
  docs/
  ├── introduction.mdx
  └── quickstart.mdx
  ```
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Upload Markdown Files">
  Drag and drop `.md` or `.mdx` files directly into your workspace root.
</Expandable>
